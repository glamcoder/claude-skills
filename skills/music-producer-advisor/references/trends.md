# Trends & Virality Reference

> Note: Trends move fast. Use this as a directional guide, not gospel. When in doubt, prioritize timeless craft over trend-chasing.

---

## What's Working Now (2024–2025)

### Synthwave / Retrowave
- **80s nostalgia is still strong** but shifting toward "neo-synthwave" — blending retrowave with modern production clarity
- Heavy reverb gated snare remains the genre signature — don't remove it, it's identity
- Melodic hooks are essential — tracks without a strong 8-bar melody struggle to travel
- **TikTok/Reels format:** The "drop" (full arrangement entry) should hit by 20–30 seconds
- Collaborations with vocalist content creators perform well
- Trending sub-niche: darksynth (heavier, more industrial synthwave) — Perturbator, Carpenter Brut influence

### Techno / Dark House
- **Minimal, hypnotic dark techno** is very active (Club scene + online)
- Organic/acoustic elements mixed with electronic (field recordings, real percussion) trending upward
- "DJ tool" format (long intro/outro, 6–8 min) still relevant for SoundCloud/Beatport
- For social media: 60-second clips showing the gear setup performing the track — hardware content performs well
- Sub-genre growing: **industrial techno** — harder, noisier, influenced by EBM

### Ambient / Experimental
- **Functional ambient** (focus, sleep, study) dominates streaming numbers — YouTube especially
- "AI ambient" backlash is real — human, organic, imperfect sounds are valued
- Spatial audio (Dolby Atmos) growing in streaming — worth considering long term
- Instagram/Reels: short aesthetic mood pieces (30–60s) with strong visual pairing
- Trending: **dark ambient** / horror-adjacent textures — niche but dedicated fanbase

### Lo-fi Hip-hop
- Market is somewhat saturated — differentiation needed
- "Lo-fi but with live elements" trending — real instruments, field recordings, live drum recording
- Emotional/narrative tracks (samples of conversations, rain, ambience) outperform pure beat loops
- **TikTok format:** The first 3 seconds must establish the vibe — no long intros
- Growing sub-niche: **lo-fi jazz** (more complex harmony, real bass recordings)
- Beat tapes (10–15 tracks as an album) still a viable format on Bandcamp

## Platform-Specific Notes

### YouTube
- Long-form ambient/lo-fi (1hr+) gets passive listener traffic — great for building catalog
- Title format that works: "[Mood] [Genre] Mix — [Activity]" e.g. "Dark Synthwave Mix — Late Night Drive"
- Consistency matters more than individual viral moments

### TikTok / Instagram Reels
- First 2–3 seconds are everything
- Hardware setup videos (showing the OP-Z, EP-40 running) get good engagement — people love seeing physical gear
- "Making a beat in X minutes" format works well for educational angle
- Sound snippets under 15 seconds with a strong hook are most shareable

### SoundCloud / Bandcamp
- Techno/house audience is still active on SoundCloud
- Bandcamp Fridays drive actual sales — release timing matters
- Tagging matters a lot on SoundCloud — use genre + mood + BPM tags

## The Evergreen Principle

Trends come and go. These qualities are timeless and always work:

1. **A strong, memorable melodic hook** (even in "hookless" genres, there's a memorable element)
2. **Emotional resonance** — the track makes the listener feel something specific
3. **Contrast** — quiet/loud, sparse/dense, simple/complex moments within a track
4. **Sonic identity** — a unique sound or texture that's identifiably "yours"
5. **Intentional imperfection** — small timing variations, humanized elements, analog warmth

When in doubt: make the track you'd want to listen to at 2am.
