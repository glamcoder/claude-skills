# Genre Reference Guide

## Synthwave / Retrowave

**BPM:** 95–115  
**Key signatures:** Minor keys dominate — Am, Dm, Em. Occasionally major for uplifting anthems.  
**Scales:** Natural Minor, Phrygian Dominant for darker tracks, Major for nostalgic warmth  

**Signature sounds:**
- Arpeggiated polysynth (OP-Z chord track with fast arp)
- Gated reverb snare (snare on 2 & 4, heavy room reverb)
- Punchy analog kick
- Lush pad underneath everything (J-6 sustained chord)
- Lead synth melody with portamento/glide

**Chord progressions:**
- i – VI – III – VII (Am – F – C – G) — the classic
- i – iv – i – V (Am – Dm – Am – E) — darker, more tension
- I – V – vi – IV (C – G – Am – F) — uplifting, major key anthem

**Drum pattern (4/4, 110 BPM):**
```
Kick:   X . . . X . . . X . . . X . . .
Snare:  . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
HiHat:  X X X X X X X X X X X X X X X X  (16ths, velocity variation)
Clap:   . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
```

**Arrangement tip:** Start with the arpeggiated synth alone for 16–32 bars before drums enter. Listeners expect the slow build. Chorus should feel like a "lift" — add octave bass and full chords simultaneously.

**Virality notes:** 80s nostalgia hooks well on TikTok/Reels. 30-second hook should land by 0:30. Gated reverb snare is an instant genre signal. Neon/retro visual pairing amplifies reach.

## Techno / Dark House

**BPM:** 125–145 (Techno), 120–130 (Dark House)  
**Key signatures:** Often minimal harmonic content — single notes, drones, power chords. When chords exist: Phrygian, minor.  
**Scales:** Phrygian, Phrygian Dominant, Natural Minor  

**Signature sounds:**
- Distorted/compressed kick (the most important element — everything serves the kick)
- Clicky or distorted 16th-note hi-hats with swing
- Sub bass or growling bassline (Dorian or Phrygian)
- Sparse chord stabs with short attack/decay
- Industrial textures, risers, noise sweeps

**Chord progressions (minimal):**
- Am – G (just two chords, repeated hypnotically)
- Drone on A, with occasional Bb or F for tension
- Single-note bassline movement is more important than chords

**Drum pattern (4/4, 132 BPM):**
```
Kick:   X . . . X . . . X . . . X . . .   (four-on-the-floor)
Snare:  . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
HiHat:  . X . X . X . X . X . X . X . X   (8ths, or 16ths with swing)
Perc:   . . X . . . . X . . X . . . . .   (offbeat, adds groove)
```

**Arrangement tip:** Techno arrangements are long (5–8 min in club context). For home production, think in 8-bar sections. Build tension by adding elements for 32 bars, then ruthlessly strip back. Silence and space are as powerful as adding layers.

**Virality notes:** Less TikTok-native, more YouTube mix/playlist-driven. Short clips (60s) with a strong drop moment work for social. "Dark and moody aesthetic" reels pair well. Prioritize feel over hooks.

## Ambient / Experimental

**BPM:** 60–90, or entirely free (no tempo grid)  
**Key signatures:** Open — major 7ths, suspended chords, add9s preferred over pure triads  
**Scales:** Major (for calm), Lydian (for floating, dreamlike), Dorian (for melancholic warmth), or chromatic exploration  

**Signature sounds:**
- Long, slow-attack pads (J-6 with high reverb and slow attack)
- Field recordings or processed samples (EP-40 stretched/pitched)
- Sparse, slow melody with long note durations
- Deep reverb and delay on everything
- Subtle movement via filter automation, not new notes

**Chord progressions:**
- Imaj7 sustained for 8+ bars — let it breathe
- I – IIm7 – IV (gentle floating movement)
- Random/modal — experiment without caring about "correct" progressions
- Clusters and dissonance work if supported by texture

**Drum pattern:** Optional. If present:
```
Kick:   X . . . . . . . X . . . . . . .   (sparse, half-time feel)
Brush:  soft texture, low velocity throughout
Rim:    . . . . . . . X . . . . . . . .   (unexpected accent)
```

**Arrangement tip:** Think in dynamics, not structure. Start quiet, build texture slowly over 2–3 minutes, reach a peak of density, then dissolve. There's no chorus. Movement comes from subtle automation: filter opens, reverb increases, a new texture fades in.

**Virality notes:** Ambient does well as "study/focus/sleep" content on YouTube (long-form, 1hr+). Short ambient clips work on Instagram as aesthetic mood pieces. Unique, otherworldly textures get shared more than conventional sounds.

## Hip-hop / Lo-fi Beats

**BPM:** 70–95  
**Key signatures:** Minor keys predominantly — Am, Gm, Cm. Jazz-influenced extensions.  
**Scales:** Dorian (warm, jazzy), Natural Minor, Blues scale  

**Signature sounds:**
- Chopped and pitch-shifted samples (EP-40 is perfect for this)
- Dusty, compressed drum samples with vinyl crackle
- Rhodes or electric piano feel (J-6 can approximate)
- Simple bassline following root notes
- Heavy sidechaining or pump if more modern/trap-influenced

**Chord progressions:**
- IIm7 – V7 – Imaj7 – VIm7 (classic jazz loop, very lo-fi friendly)
- i – VII – VI – VII (Am – G – F – G — simple, effective)
- i – iv – i – v (Am – Dm – Am – Em — melancholic)

**Drum pattern (4/4, 85 BPM, with swing):**
```
Kick:   X . . . . . X . X . . . . . . .   (syncopated)
Snare:  . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
HiHat:  X . X X . X X . X . X X . X X .   (triplet swing feel, vary velocity)
Clap:   . . . . X . . . . . . . X . . .
```

**Arrangement tip:** Lo-fi thrives on loops — a great 4-bar loop repeated with subtle variation is enough. Add variation every 8 bars: mute an element, change a hi-hat pattern, add a one-bar fill. Keep it minimal. The vibe IS the track.

**Virality notes:** Study/chill playlists on YouTube are the main channel. Lo-fi girl aesthetic (visual pairing) is still strong. For social: 15–30 second clips with strong visual + recognizable chord loop. Sample-based feel (even if it's not sampled) performs better than clean/pristine production.

## Cross-Genre Tips

### Combining genres (hybrid ideas)
- **Lo-fi Techno:** 110–120 BPM, dusty drums from EP-40, dark minor chords, no melody — hypnotic
- **Synthwave Ambient:** slow synthwave chord progression (70 BPM), no drums, lush J-6 pads, OP-Z arp as texture
- **Afro-House adjacent:** 120–125 BPM, Dorian scale, syncopated percussion on EP-40, J-6 chord stabs on offbeats

### The "one thing that makes it interesting" principle
Every good track has one element that surprises or hooks. Before finishing, ask: "What's the one thing here that someone would tell a friend about?" If the answer is "nothing," add one unexpected element — an unusual chord, a sound design moment, a structural break.
